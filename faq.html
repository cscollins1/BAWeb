  • What can I expect after I register?

    iGrade

    After all of the required documents have been submitted and accepted you will then need to activate your iGrade accounts through links sent through your provided email addresses. Each student will have their own iGrade account and each student must have at least one parent account linked to their student account. A family with multiple students registered at Bridge Academy will have one (or more) parent account that will link to each student account. As an example, a family with two students will have 3 accounts. Parents will be able to log on to their parent account and easily toggle between their student accounts. Students may not view their siblings accounts.

  • How many credits does my home-educated student need to graduate?

    There is no specific number of credits a homeschooler must have to graduate. It is up to the parent to decide when the course of study for the student is completed. At that point, the parent will issue a completed transcript (a record of grades 9-12) and a parent-signed homeschool diploma. If you chose, you could use public school credit standards as a guide, but they are not required for homeschool students. Colleges are accustomed to receiving different numbers of credits from school districts in multiple states. College admissions officers will be looking at the types of courses the student completed–easy or difficult, along with grades, extracurricular activities, and the student’s SAT or ACT scores. Requirements differ from state colleges to private colleges–some schools are more selective than others. You will find admission requirements on college websites. This could help you plan your high school program.

    From Home Educators Association of Virginia - www.heav.org

  • Will my child have homework?

    Bridge Academy is University Modeled, in that your student will be expected to attend their classes every weekand may be required to complete assignments for each class. In most cases your student should spend some time each day completing assignments for the classes they are enrolled in. Homework will vary depending on the class type and grade level. Core classes such as English, Math, History, and Science will require more dedicated time than that of electives such as Culinary Arts, P.E., or Art. Determining the exact amount of time your student will spend on each subject is very difficult as it varies depending on the dificulty of each class. Fell free to reach out to the teacher of any specific class to get a better idea of the required workload of their class.

  • As a parent, what is expected of me if my child is enrolled at Bridge Academy?

    Bridge Academy is here to help support you in your home schooling needs, we are not here to replace you! We fully expect you to remain engaged and the head of your student's academic needs while your student is enrolled in classes with us at Bridge Academy. Topics and lessons are presented in class but class time with your student is limited. This means that while at home your student may need additional help. Your student's teacher may be able to help or direct you to additional resources as needed if given ample time to do so. Please do not reach out to your student's teacher late Thursday for help on an assignment that is due Friday.

    Fundraising and volunteer hours are not required, however, there will likely be opportunities to volunteer for a variety of tasks throughout the year if you so choose.

  • How do I get textbooks (purchase or lending library)?

    Parents are responsible for obtaining textbooks and other school supplies for their student. We are mindful of the financial sacrifices families make to home-educate their students and put a great deal of effort into choosing curriculum that is both academically strong as well as as cost effective. Bellow you will find a list of a few ways you may choose to obtain the required textbooks.

    Bridge Academy Lending Library

    Books, if available, may be rented for the year for a $5 non-refundable fee and a post dated check for the replacement value of the book. When the book is returned at the end of the year, the post dated check will be returned to you. If you do not return the book, your check will be cashed and the book you rented will be replaced. Books are available on a first come, first served basis. We do NOT have enough books for all students in all classes. Hedi Juback is our Lending Librarian and can be reached at heidijuback@gmail.com.

    Used Textbooks

    Used curriculum sales in the area are a great way to find books for your student. Bridge Academy hopes to have another UCS if COVID guidelines permit. If so, information will be available on the website in the Spring.

    Bridge Academy Book Exchange - PUBLIC Facebook page

    New Textbook

    Christian Bookstore as well as many other online websites offer textbooks. Check the course description pages, as your student's instructor may have posted a link to the reccomended textbook.

    If you have difficulty in finding a required textbook, please contact the teacher of the class the book is required for. They can often most easily direct you.
